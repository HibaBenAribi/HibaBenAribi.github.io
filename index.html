About

Hello !

Here’s a summary of my career and research experiences:



I hold a research master's degree in Neuroscience and Biotechnology, with my thesis focusing on the epigenomic changes in multiple sclerosis patients within the Tunisian population. As part of this work, I completed a one-year research internship, where I conducted genomics studies, managed donor relations for scientific research, and performed blood collection, DNA sequencing, and bioinformatics analysis of metagenomic data.

Following my graduation, I worked as a Pre-Doctoral researcher for an international research project, PRIMA PROMEDLIFE. My research focused on biochemically characterizing Tunisian species, particularly their polyphenolic content and antioxidant activity.

I also conducted in vivo studies on laboratory rats to assess the physiological and behavioral effects of specific diets, including the execution of behavioral tests.

My involvement extended to developing a Tunisian AI-based web platform, where I created an integrative interface that provided access to a database containing data on hundreds of Tunisian plants and dishes.

I have extensive experience in bioinformatics, particularly in the analysis of multiomics data, including DNA, RNA, methylation, and microRNA. I have developed novel tools for multiomics data analysis, including R and Python libraries, and have contributed to the development of open-source web-based tools. Most of these tools are the subject of peer-reviewed publications.

To further enhance my expertise and expand my professional network, I have actively participated in numerous hackathons, workshops, and conferences, building a strong international presence in the bioinformatics field.
